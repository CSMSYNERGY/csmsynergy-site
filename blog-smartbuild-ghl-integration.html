SmartBuild and GoHighLevel: Why These Two Tools Are Better Together

SmartBuild is great for configuring and quoting buildings. GoHighLevel is great for managing leads and automating follow-up. What happens when you connect them? You get a system that runs your sales process from first inquiry to signed deposit — without anyone having to babysit every step.

Carolyn Miller
Founder, CSM Synergy · $150M+ in Construction Sales

Most shed dealers and post frame builders who use SmartBuild love it for the same reasons: it makes configuring a building fast, the visual output looks professional, and quoting becomes something a salesperson can do in front of a customer instead of three days later. SmartBuild solves a real problem.

But SmartBuild only covers one part of your sales process — the quoting part. What happens before the quote (lead capture, follow-up, qualification) and after the quote (the follow-up sequence, the pipeline, the conversion) is still largely left to memory, spreadsheets, and whoever happens to be working that day.

That's the gap. And it's costing more deals than most dealers realize.

GoHighLevel (GHL) is built to fill exactly that gap — and when it's connected to SmartBuild, the two tools cover the entire sales cycle end-to-end. Here's how each one does its job, and what changes when you connect them.

What Each Tool Does Best

SmartBuild
  • Visual building configuration
  • Accurate, professional quotes in minutes
  • 3D rendering customers can see
  • Pricing tied to your actual cost structure
  • Order and production documentation
  • Spec sheets and cut lists for the build crew
GoHighLevel
  • Lead capture from all sources (web, ads, calls)
  • Automated text and email follow-up
  • Visual sales pipeline by stage
  • Two-way SMS conversations with leads
  • Review request automation
  • Reporting on close rates and lead sources

The pattern is clear: SmartBuild owns the configuration and quote. GoHighLevel owns the lead and the relationship. Together, they handle the entire journey from "stranger submits a form" to "customer signs and pays a deposit."

The Gap Most Dealers Have Right Now

Without integration, here's what the typical process looks like: A lead comes in through your website or Facebook. Someone writes it down, sends a quick reply, or maybe enters it into a spreadsheet. Eventually a rep gets back to them, asks what they need, and then opens SmartBuild to build a quote. The quote gets emailed as a PDF. Then the rep waits.

If the customer doesn't respond within a day or two, follow-up depends entirely on whether the rep remembers to reach back out. Many don't — not because they're lazy, but because they have 20 other quotes in progress, three customers on the lot, and a manager asking about someone else's order. The lead goes cold. The sale goes to whoever called second.

"The quote is not the finish line — it's the starting gun. The race to close begins the moment you hit send, and most dealers aren't even in the race."

This is the gap. And it's not a people problem. It's a systems problem. Your rep isn't failing to follow up because they don't care — they're failing because there's no system that makes follow-up happen automatically when they don't.

What the Connected Workflow Looks Like

Here's how SmartBuild and GoHighLevel work together in a properly integrated setup:

Step 1 — Lead comes in. Whether it's a web form, a Facebook lead ad, an inbound call, or a lot walk-in entered by the rep, the lead is captured in GoHighLevel automatically. A contact record is created, the lead source is tagged, and a follow-up sequence starts immediately — a welcome text goes out within minutes.

Step 2 — Rep builds the quote in SmartBuild. When the rep sits down with the customer (in person or by phone), they configure the building in SmartBuild as always. The quote is generated and sent to the customer.

Step 3 — Pipeline stage updates in GHL. The contact is moved to "Quote Sent" in the GoHighLevel pipeline. This triggers a new automation: a follow-up text goes out 48 hours later checking if they received the quote and have any questions. Another touch fires at day 5. Another at day 10. None of these require the rep to remember anything.

Step 4 — Two-way conversation stays in GHL. When the customer replies — whether by text, email, or a call logged by the rep — everything lives in the contact record. Any rep on the team can pick up the conversation without starting from zero. No more "I'm not sure where Mike left off with this one."

Step 5 — Deal closes, review request fires. When the contact moves to "Won" in the pipeline, GHL automatically sends a review request to the customer. Project delivery milestones can trigger satisfaction check-ins. The relationship doesn't end at the sale.

You don't have to choose between SmartBuild and GoHighLevel. Dealers who try to replace SmartBuild with GoHighLevel for quoting end up frustrated — GHL is not a configurator. Dealers who use SmartBuild alone miss the pipeline and automation GHL provides. The right answer is both, connected, each doing what it does best.

What Changes When the System Is Running

When dealers move from a disconnected setup to an integrated SmartBuild + GoHighLevel workflow, the shifts are consistent:

  • Response time drops to minutes, not hours. The first text goes out automatically — no rep has to remember to send it. Studies consistently show that response time in the first hour dramatically increases close rates. Automation makes speed effortless.
  • No lead gets lost. Every inquiry that comes in has a contact record, a stage in the pipeline, and an active automation sequence. "Falling through the cracks" becomes structurally impossible.
  • Close rates on quoted deals go up. The difference between a 20% close rate and a 35% close rate is often just consistent follow-up. When the system does it automatically, every quoted lead gets the same disciplined attention regardless of how busy the lot is.
  • Managers can actually see the pipeline. Instead of asking reps for a status update and getting a vague answer, the pipeline view shows every active deal, its stage, and when it was last touched. A 10-minute pipeline review replaces an hour of back-and-forth.
  • Reviews accumulate without asking. The automated review request at job completion generates a steady stream of Google reviews — which feeds local SEO and builds social proof that keeps more leads coming in.

The result is a sales process that runs at a consistent level regardless of who is working, how busy the season is, or whether the owner is on the lot. That's what a system is supposed to do.
