Is This You?

You Built a Good Business. Now You're Ready to Lead a Great One.

Most construction business owners are exceptional at their craft. They know how to build. They know their product. But somewhere between $500K and $5M, the job changes — and the skills that got you here aren't always the ones that get you to the next level.

Leadership isn't something you either have or don't. It's a skill. And like any skill, it gets better when you practice it intentionally — with the right guidance and the right people around you.

  • Your team depends on you for everything — and you're tired of being the bottleneck in your own business.

  • You have good people but struggle to get consistent performance out of them — and you're not sure what you're missing.

  • You're working harder than ever but the business doesn't seem to grow without you pushing it every single day.

  • You want a space to think and talk through real challenges with other owners who actually understand construction — not a generic mastermind.

What's Inside

What Happens on Every Call

This isn't a presentation you sit through — it's a working session. Every call has a purpose, a structure, and a commitment. You leave with clarity, a plan, and people holding you to it.

Every Call — Part 1

Issue Solving

You bring a real, current issue from your business. The group — led by Carolyn — collaborates on it together. No canned advice, no generic frameworks. Real problems, real people, real solutions developed in the room.

Every Call — Part 2

Goal Check-In & Accountability

Every member sets clear, specific goals between calls. When we come back together, you report on what you said you'd do. The group celebrates wins and works through roadblocks — together. No more setting goals you quietly abandon.

Every Call — Part 3

Leadership Development

Each session includes focused discussion on a leadership topic relevant to where construction business owners actually are — team building, communication, decisions under pressure, culture, and resilience. Practical, not theoretical.

How It Works

A Biweekly Working Session — Not a Webinar

This is a working session, not a presentation. Every 90 minutes is structured with purpose — issue solving, accountability, and leadership development. You show up, you participate, you leave with something actionable.

The format is intentionally small — because the real value comes from the conversation, not a slide deck. You'll know the other owners in your cohort. You'll hear their issues. You'll celebrate their wins. Real accountability requires real relationships.
